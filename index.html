Who Exactly is Ed Sheeran?

Ed Christopher Sheeran was born in February 17, 1991 in England. To be more exact, he was born in West Yorkshire and later moved to Suffolk. His father was a curator when he was young, and came from an Irish family. His mother is a music teacher, and he does have a brother who is a composer. Clearly, he has a family full of talent. This will be all the information given regarding his family as he is a private person, and there is no need for anyone to know the details of his family members out of respect. Ed Sheeran started singing when he was four for a local church choir, then started to learn guitar and cello as well. By the time he was in high school, he started to write songs. In 2008, he started at the Academy of Contemporary Music (ACM) to study music, but he left that very same year to take a shot at his career instead. Some of his biggest musical influences include Eric Clapton, Eminem, Van Morrison, The Beatles, Slipknot, and Bring Me the Horizon.

Music Career

Prior to 2011

While the mainstream fans know Ed Sheeran for his songs on the main music streaming services, many of his old fans know him through his live shows that he did prior to his rise to fame. Prior to the Academy of Contemporary Music, Ed Sheeran was already recording his first EP at the age of thirteen. There are 20 copies of this in the world, and he has 19 copies. Because they were made at such a young age, he said he does not want anyone to have a hold of them. Of course, all it takes is the one copy that he does not have and the advanced digital world to let the whole world full access to the EP. By the time he turned seventeen, he was already erforming at small venues. When Ed left ACM in 2008, he went to support another artist and by 2009, he had released another EP called You Need Me. On February 28, 2010, he was seventeen at the time, SB.TV posted a video of Ed Sheeran performing the acoustic version of "You Need Me, I Don't Need You" in a recording studio, which is in his first album, "Plus", which was not released until 2011. Ed Sheeran has accreditted SB.TV and Jamal Edwards (1990-2022) for his success, as it was the breakthrough moment for him. SB.TV was formed by a small group of team to give different underground artists a chance to shine, such as Ed Sheera, Jessie J, and Rita Ora. SB.TV founder, Jamal Edwards, and Ed Sheeran remained close friends until Jamal passed in 2022. After his video was posted on SB.TV, he went on tour with a rapper named Example. Later in the same year of 2010, he released his another independent EP, Loose Change, with featured the hit single "The A Team". (The EP was then re-released later under Assylum/Atlantic Records, with "Let It Out" replacing "The A Team", as it became the lead single in his "Plus" album.) With digital media allowing greater access to music platform, that very same video on SB.TV increased his fan base, and released two more EPs featuring songs that he collaborated with Amy Wadge, who later in the years co-wrote "Thinking Out Loud", and won 2016 Grammy Awards for Song of the Year. Ed's career started to expand in 2010 as he was invited to play at Jamie Foxx's club, The Foxxhole. Ed did not know anyone in the area at the time, so Jamie Foxx let him stay at the house during the time. With his recognition increasing over the year of 2010, he continued to released another independent EP, No. 5 Collaborations Project, in January of 2011. Every song on this EP was a collaboration with different hip hop artists including Wiley, Ghetts, Devlin, P Money, JME, etc. This EP became the first independent record to reach No.2 on Itunes chart without any promotion or big label to back it up. With this momentum, Ed Sheeran put on one free show for fans three months after. However, with over 1,000 fans showed up, Ed decided to put on a total of four different shows to make sure every fan got the same experience. One of the shows was on the street outside the venue after it was closed. At the end of that month, he was signed to Assylum Records/Atlantic Records.

Time of "+" (Plus) : 2011-2013

In April of 2011, Ed performed and introduced "The A Song" on a BBC music show as his debut single. Despite it being on "Loose Change", "The A Song" then got introduced to the rest of the world and it took over the charts worldwide. He then released "You Need Me, I Don't Need You", "Lego House", and "Drunk", along with their music videos from the album. All of these songs also ended up on the top ten of various music charts worldwide. On September 12, 2011, his first full album, "+" (Plus) was released. This album debuted at No.1 on the UK Albums Chart, and became the second best-selling debut album in the UK for 2011. In addition, the album was certified platnium six times by the British Phonograpy Industry as well. As a result, Ed Sheeran took home the 2012 Brit Awards for Best Male Solo Artist, and Best Breakthrough Act. The hit single, "The A Team", from the album also was nominated for Song of the Year at the 2013 Grammy Awards. Prior to the 2013 Grammy Awards, there was a suggestion that Ed could perform his song during the show. However, the suggestion received blacklash from the Grammy association with the reason that he was not high-profile enough to perform. To solve this issue, Elton John offerred to perform with Ed Sheeran on stage. Together, they performed his single for the show. Aside from working on his own music, Ed Sheeran also co-wrote "Moment" on One Direction debut album in 2011, along with "Little Things" in their album "Take Me Home" in 2012. He then continued to co-wrote songs with other artists such as Taylor Swift on "Everything Has Changed" on her Red album and was invited to go on her arenas and stadiums tour in 2013 as a headliner. During this period of time, some of his songs such as "Give Me Love" and "I See Fire" were featured on "Vampire Diaries" and "The Hobbit: The Desolation of Smaug". He was then nominated for Best New Artist at the 2014 Grammy Awards, but the award was taken home by Sam Smith instead.

Time of "x" (Multiply) : 2014-2015

On June 23,2014, Ed Sheeran had realease his second full album, "x" (Multiply). He released "Sing" first to the public prior to the album release, but it was different from what his fans were used to, so he followed up with "One", which is an acoustic song. However,to his surprise, "Sing" became his first No.1 single in the UK. Between 2011 (when he released "+") and 2014, he had written 120 songs total, and only 16 made it to the full album. With the success of some of the songs from the album, such as "Don't", "Thinking Out Loud", "Photograph", and "Sing", this album took No.1 spot in both US Billboard Top 200 and UK Albums Chart. With this album released, he had his first tour. In 2014, Spotify announced that Ed Sheeran was the most streamed artist of the year, and his album "x" was the most streamed album of the year. In the same year, "x" was nominated for the 57th Grammy's Album of the Year and Best Pop Vocal Album, while "I See Fire" was nominated for Best Song Written for Visual Media. However, a the 58th Grammy Awards, "Thinking Out Loud" took home Song of the Year and Best Pop Solo Performance, while also nominated for Best Record of the Year. This album ended up being the second best selling abum worldwide in 2015. Among the songs that he had written, "Love Yourself" was on that list. Ultimately, this song was given to Justin Bieber for his album in 2015. Ed stated that the song would have been scrapped since it was not going to make it to the upcoming album list. During this period of time between 2014 and 2015, he has received so much recognition and fame came to quickly at an exponential rate that he was starting to struggle mentally. He was told to wear contacts instead of his glasses, fix his hair, wear certain clothes, and he ended up with social anxiety. As a result, he made an announcement that he would step away from social media to enjoy life and the things he missed out on with his loved ones. In March of 2015, Ed Sheeran founded a new record label, Gingerbread Man Records, with Warner Music. Shortly after, he signed his first aritst, Jamie Lawson, who is a folk singer. He then signed Foy Vance, another folk artist who he admired, and most recently, Massie Peters.

Time of "รท" (Divide) : 2016-2018

After a year of hiatus, Ed made a comeback on social media simply to announced the release of his new album, "รท" (Divide). There are sixteen songs on this album, and some of the hit songs include "Castle on the Hil", "Shape of You", "Perfect", and "How Would You Feel(Paean)". As most of Ed's songs are related to what he sees everyday, the songs from this album are a more personal than previous albums. Some of the songs are about his relationship with his childhood friends ("Castle on the Hill"), wife ("Pefect"), his grandparent's love story ("Nancy Mulligan"), his grandmother passing from his mom's point of view ("Supermarket Flowers"), and as someone who struggled mentally from society ("Save Myself"). On January of 2017, "Castle on the Hill" and "Shape of You" ranked No.1 and No.2 of UK Singles Chart, and it was the first time in history that an artist achieved this with new songs. Similarly, he made history on both the German Singles Chart and Australian Recording Industry Association (ARIA) Music Chart. Later within that week, Ed made history again as the first artist to have two debut songs making top 10 on the Billboard Hot 100 chart. Ed Sheeran went on tour for this album as well, and he also had the opportunity to collaborate with Taylor Swift on her album again, performed a duet with Beyonce, and also worked with Eminem. At the 60th Grammy Awards, Ed Sheeran won Best Pop Vocal Album for "รท" (Divide) and Best Pop Solo Performance for "Shape of You". "Shape of You" became the best selling single of 2017 in the UK and the US. Meanwhile, the album itself also became the best selling album of 2017 in both countries as well. In 2018, the International Federation of the Phonographic Industry (IFPI) had name him the Global Recording Artist of the Year for being the best selling artist worldwide.

No. 6 Collaborations Project and Time of "=" (Equal) : 2019-2022

On May of 2019, Ed started to release singsles from his No. 6 Collaborations Project. Many of the more recent fans and the general public would consider this as an ablum, but it is a project. Ed had other collabotion projects prior to this, and for Ed, these collaborations project gives him a chance to have fun and work with different people to try different things musically that he would not do on his own music. Some of the collaborations on this project include Camila Cabello, Eminem, 50 Cent, Travis Scott, Justin Biebeer, Chris Stapleton, Bruno Mars, etc. At the end of the decade, Spotify had named him the second most streaming artist of the decade. In December of 2020, he released "Afterglow" as a single after his first child. In June 2021, he released "Bad Habits" as a title song to his album, "=" (Equal), which as released on October of 2021. In this album, some of the songs reflect the changes in his life during pandemic lockdown, such as marriage, having a child, and the lost of a friend. He had painted the album cover and promoted "Visiting Hours", which was a tribute to his friend, Michael Gudinski, who was a leading figure in the Australian music industry. Ed mentioned that it was the first time he had the proper time to grief, and that it is one of the most important songs on the album for him. On November of 2021, Ed Sheeran collaborated with Elton John for a Christmas song, "Merry Christmas", for charity and this song topped the UK chart, making it his 12th chart-topper. In 2022, Ed Sheeran had released a duet with Taylor Swift that they had written years before, called "The Joker and the Queen". He also collaborated with other artists such as Camila Cabello, J Balvin, and Lil Baby. For his song, "2step" , which originally featured Lil Baby, he did another version of the song featuring Ukranian pop-rock band Antytila. With this remix, any royalties from the streams for the year were donated to Music Saves UA. Furthermore, he released a music video for the remix with clips that the band front man filmed during this time as a soldier. This might be the first time the topic of charity work and Ed Sheeran has come to the spotlight, but he is known for contributing to charity work without the limelight.

Personsal Life

Ed Sheeran is known for his private life, as he does not care for the glamour considering how many couches he had crash on while doing live shows before he was recognized. In 2011, Ed Sheeran bought a farm in Suffolk, which is where he was raised. After being in a few private relationships between 2011 and 2015, Ed started his romantic relationship with a childhood friend. After three years, they were engaged, and married a year after that. In 2020, they had their first daughter, and the second daughter was bornin 2022. During the later time of the pregnancy and after the daughters were borned, Ed made sure to take time off to spend time with his family and to not miss any special moments. Ed had expand the farm to accommodate to the growing family while focusing on doing charity work in his hometown. He donated thirteen bags of clothes to St Elizabeth Hospice charity shop, and some of the items were put up for auction to raise more money for the children's hospice. He also launched his own music foundation in his hometown to help artists under the age of 18, and donated money to his high school for any necessary items that the school might need. During the pandemic time period, Ed created his own charity for Suffolk to donate and raise money for local charities and children's hospital ward in response to COVID-19. Both of his parents also organized a fundraiser to support local charities that assist with children with dissabilities and special education needs. Beisdes his hometown, Ed has also participated in fundraisers for charities with different purposes such as street sex workers, West African Ebola virus epidemic, and more.

Why Ed Sheeran?

Many people focus on the physical attractiveness of a celebrity, and while I do not disagree that it is not a factor (unfortunately in this society), I think the reason why I find Ed so endearing is his humbleness. A person can be full of talent, but it's the values and charateristics of a person that holds long-lasting relationships. Of course, I do not know him personally, but I find him very down to earth. I attended one of his concerts during the "x" (Multiply) tour and it was the best concert I have attended. It was purely a one man show - just him, the guitar, and a loop pedal. He had mentioned that he wanted to keep the ticket pricing as low as he could, so he tickets were less than $100 for extremely good seats, and a little over $100 for the floor section. Rather than a concert, it felt like a jam session, with thousands of people singing to every song without any craziness. Even now, he is still putting on small intimate shows to test his new songs and playing unreleased songs. I think at the end of the day, he went against all suggestions on how he SHOULD look, and stick with the typical Ed Sheeran attire - t-shirt, jeans, pair of glasses, and his messy ginger hair.
